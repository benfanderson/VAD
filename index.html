1. WHO IS ELIGIBLE?

To be eligible, a person must be facing probable death within six months, or 12 months for those suffering a neurogenerative disease. They must be at least 18 years old.

2. FIRST REQUEST

The person must make a request to a doctor, most likely a GP or a specialist. The doctor sends this formal first request to the Voluntary Assisted Dying Board. The patient will make three requests in all: two oral and one written. Each request must be clear and unambiguous. the Board is notified at every step along the way.

3. FIRST ASSESSMENT

The doctor assess the paitient and gives a diagnosis, treatment options and likely outcomes. The patient is told of the potential risks of the VAD medication being administered. The doctor needs to be satisfied the patient is capapable of making a Voluntary decision without coercion. The doctor will have already undergone a training course before making the assessment.

4. REFERRAL TO A SECOND DOCTOR

The first doctor - called the Co-ordinating Practicioner - makes a referral to a second doctor. This doctor is called the Consulting Practicioner.

5. SECOND ASSESSMENT

The second doctor (Consulting Practicioner) assesses the patient and makes a diagnosis.

6. WRITTEN STATEMENT

If the second doctor approves, the patient makes a voluntary written statement VAD.

7. WITNESSES

The statement needs to be witnessed and signed by two independent witnesses. They cannot have any financial interest in the outcome of the patient's death. They cannot be family members.

8. THE FINAL REQUEST

The final request form from the dying patient is sent to the Voluntary Review Board.

9. FINAL REVIEW

A final review is carried out by the original Co-ordinating Practicioner. The patient now has formal approval to get the medication.

10. MEDICATION

A decision made on how the medication will be administered. If the patient can self-administer then they must appoint a Contact Person. If not, the medication will be administered by a practicioner.

11. PRESCRIPTION

The Co-ordinating Practicioner prescribes the medication.

12. SUPPLY

An authorised supplier dispenses the medication for the patient.

13. THE FINAL DAY

If they are able, the person will self-administer. If not, a witness is required to watch the practicioner administer it.

15. MEDICATION MANAGEMENT

After the person's death (by any cause) either the Administering Practioner disposes of the unused medication as soon as practicable or the Contact Person must return to the Authorised Disposer within 14 days.

16. DISPOSAL

Authorised disposer disposes of unused medication.
